## Business Plan (Execution Version)

## 1) Positioning
The AI Update is a practical AI intelligence brand for regular people, solo operators, and small teams that want clear outcomes, not hype. The differentiator is translation: converting fast AI news into usable actions with low-cost tools and realistic workflows.

## 2) Audience Segments
Primary audience is non-technical builders and small business owners who need better output without enterprise budgets.
Secondary audience is technical-curious creators and operators who want implementation guidance they can use this week.
Tertiary audience is teams evaluating AI process upgrades for support, content, marketing, and operations.

## 3) Core Offer Stack
Free layer is high-frequency update posts, beginner-friendly breakdowns, and implementation checklists.
Growth layer is the Zero-Dollar AI Toolkit lead magnet plus weekly curated newsletter.
Paid layer is a premium intelligence subscription with deep-dive operator briefs and implementation playbooks.
Service layer is B2B implementation consulting for workflow design, deployment support, and optimization.

## 4) Product Content Model
Each update must answer four questions clearly: what changed, why it matters, what to test, and what to ignore.
Video sources require full transcript review before article writing.
If one source contains multiple major updates, split into multiple focused articles.
No placeholder summaries are allowed in published content.

## 5) Distribution Channels
Owned channels are website articles, guides, and lead capture pages.
Social distribution is X with a cadence focused on signal posts, short breakdown threads, and article amplification.
Newsletter distribution is weekly digest plus optional category-specific editions later (SMB ops, creator workflow, local AI).

## 6) 90-Day Targets
Reach 1,000 organic X followers with consistent update quality and stronger hooks.
Reach 500 email subscribers from website + social conversion.
Publish 60 source-grounded articles with a transcript-first standard for video-derived content.
Convert first 50 paid subscribers at $5/month.
Close first 2 consulting discovery calls from newsletter and article CTAs.

## 7) KPI Dashboard
Top-of-funnel KPIs are impressions, profile visits, site sessions, and newsletter signups.
Content KPIs are article completion, CTR to source links, and social reshare rate.
Business KPIs are subscriber conversion rate, MRR, consulting pipeline value, and retention/churn.
Quality KPIs are source coverage freshness, transcript completion rate, and manual correction rate.

## 8) Weekly Operating System
Monday is source scan + trend clustering.
Tuesday through Friday is publish cycle for high-signal updates and practical breakdowns.
Saturday is long-form evergreen guide work.
Sunday is analytics review, gap analysis, and next-week planning.
Every day includes one quality gate: source verification before publish.

## 9) Monetization Path
Phase 1 monetizes attention with lead magnet and newsletter growth.
Phase 2 monetizes trust with low-cost premium intelligence subscription.
Phase 3 monetizes outcomes with consulting packages and implementation retainers.
Pricing starts simple and increases only when measurable customer outcomes are documented.

## 10) Risk Controls
Platform dependency risk on X is reduced by growing email list and website SEO.
Model/source volatility risk is reduced by source verification and transcript-first policy.
Operational drift risk is reduced with fixed publishing standards and KPI review cadence.
Credibility risk is reduced by banning placeholders and enforcing source-linked claims.

Engine and site foundations are active.
Content quality system has been upgraded to source-grounded, transcript-first publishing.
Current focus is growth and conversion, not feature sprawl.
