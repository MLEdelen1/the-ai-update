FREE STARTER KIT

50 Practical AI Use Cases + Copy-Ready Prompts

This is built for real life, not demos. Pick one use case from your bottleneck category, paste the prompt, then run it on an actual task in under 10 minutes. Every entry includes when to use it, expected output, and a quick tip so you get better results immediately.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Operations (1-5) | Communication (6-10) | Sales (11-15) | Support (16-20) | Productivity (21-25)

Content (26-30) | Planning (31-35) | Analysis (36-40) | Personal Admin (41-45) | Small Business Growth (46-50)

Operations

1

SOP Draft Builder

Who it helps / when: Owners and team leads creating repeatable process docs.
Copy-ready prompt: "Create a step-by-step SOP for [process]. Include purpose, tools needed, exact steps, QA checks, and escalation rules. Keep it usable by a new hire on day one."
Expected output format: SOP with numbered steps and QA checklist.
Quick tip: Add current tool names and common failure points in your input.
2

Task Delegation Brief

Who it helps / when: Managers assigning work without confusion.
Copy-ready prompt: "Turn this request into a delegation brief with objective, scope, deadline, owner, dependencies, and done criteria: [paste request]."
Expected output format: One-page delegation brief template.
Quick tip: Include deadline and what good looks like.
3

Meeting to Action Tracker

Who it helps / when: Teams leaving meetings with unclear next steps.
Copy-ready prompt: "Convert these notes into action items with owner, due date, status, and blocker: [notes]. Flag missing owners or dates."
Expected output format: Action table plus risk flags.
Quick tip: Paste raw notes exactly as captured for better extraction.
4

Workflow Bottleneck Finder

Who it helps / when: Small teams missing deadlines repeatedly.
Copy-ready prompt: "Review this workflow timeline and identify top 3 bottlenecks, probable root causes, and fixes ranked by effort vs impact: [timeline]."
Expected output format: Ranked bottleneck list with suggested fixes.
Quick tip: Include rough wait times between steps.
5

Weekly Ops Review

Who it helps / when: Operators and founders running weekly reviews.
Copy-ready prompt: "Summarize this week into wins, misses, root causes, and next-week process changes with measurable targets: [log]."
Expected output format: Review summary + next-week commitments.
Quick tip: Include numbers (volume, time, error count).

Communication

6

Professional Rewriter

Who it helps / when: Anyone polishing a sensitive email or message.
Copy-ready prompt: "Rewrite this message to be clear, respectful, and concise. Keep intent, remove friction, and add one clear next step with deadline: [message]."
Expected output format: Final message + optional shorter variant.
Quick tip: State relationship (boss, client, peer) for tone accuracy.
7

Copy-ready prompt: "Help me prepare for a tough conversation about [topic]. Give opening script, likely objections, calm responses, and a close."
Expected output format: Talk track with objection-response pairs.
Quick tip: Share desired outcome so the script stays focused.
8

Copy-ready prompt: "Rewrite this update into: context, current status, blockers, ask, and ETA in under 120 words: [update]."
Expected output format: Structured status message.
Quick tip: Include deadline and who needs to act.
9

Executive Summary Writer

Copy-ready prompt: "Create an executive summary from this data and notes. Include key result, risk, decision needed, and recommended action: [data]."
Expected output format: 4-part executive brief.
Quick tip: Provide one-sentence context about the project.
10

Follow-Up Sequence Builder

Who it helps / when: Anyone waiting on approval or response.
Copy-ready prompt: "Write 3 follow-up messages for this scenario at 24h, 72h, and 7 days. Keep tone polite and include one CTA each: [scenario]."
Expected output format: Three ready-to-send follow-ups.
Quick tip: Mention what happens if no response by final date.
