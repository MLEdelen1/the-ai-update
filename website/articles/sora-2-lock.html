Executive Summary

OpenAI's Sora 2.0 has finally addressed the 'Consistency Crisis' of AI video with its 'Temporal Lock' technology. This system maintains a persistent internal memory of every object in a scene, ensuring that if a character walks behind a tree and reappears, their features remain identical. By treating the latent space as a 'Persistent 3D Volume', Sora 2.0 effectively functions as a game engine that renders from a text prompt.



For the professional workflow, the 'Canvas for Video' integration is the real game-changer. It allows editors to select specific elements of a video—like a background sign or a person's hat—and change them via text without regenerating the entire clip. This 'In-Painting for Video' provides the granular control that professional editors have been demanding, making Sora 2.0 a production-ready tool rather than just a research demo.



Strategically, Sora 2.0 is being used for 'Generative Simulation'.
