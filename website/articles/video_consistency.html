3. Technical Architecture Analysis

Kling 3.0: Volumetric Priors & Director Mode

Kling 3.0 deviates from standard pixel-diffusion by first constructing a low-fidelity Volumetric Prior (a 3D 'ghost' scene) before applying textures and lighting. - Director Mode Engine: This layer translates standard cinematic commands (e.g., 'Dolly Zoom,' 'Pan-left at 24fps') into precise camera vectors within the latent space. - Semantic Depth Mapping: Kling generates a real-time depth map during the diffusion process, ensuring that foreground objects correctly occlude background elements without the 'ghosting' common in earlier models.

Sora 2 Pro: Temporal Lock (TL) & Latent Anchors

Sora 2 Pro utilizes 'Latent Anchors' to maintain world state across long durations. - Temporal Lock Mechanism: TL identifies key semantic identifiers (the 'DNA' of a character or object) and locks their high-dimensional vector representations across all frames. This prevents the 'morphing' effect where a blue shirt gradually becomes green over 30 seconds. - Flow-Guided Attention: Sora uses a proprietary attention mechanism that weighs future frame consistency as heavily as past frame consistency, effectively 'looking ahead' to ensure that a moving car eventually reaches its destination in a physically plausible manner.
