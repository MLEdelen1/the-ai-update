# Deep Dive: How AI is Speeding Up Federal Permitting with DraftNEPABench

Building roads, power grids, and clean energy plants takes a lot of time. A huge part of that time is spent getting federal permits. Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) and OpenAI have teamed up to solve this problem. Together, they created a new tool called DraftNEPABench. 

Let us dive into how this artificial intelligence technology is helping experts review and approve infrastructure projects much faster.

## The Problem: The Slow Pace of NEPA

Before a big building project can start in the United States, it must pass a strict review. This is required by a law called the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA). 

Scientists and experts must study how a new highway or solar farm will affect the local environment. They have to answer questions like: Will it pollute the water? How will it affect local wildlife? 

Answering these questions requires writing documents that are often thousands of pages long. Experts must sort through massive amounts of environmental data. Because humans have to do this by hand, writing these reports can take years. This slows down important projects that the country needs.

## The Solution: Introducing DraftNEPABench

To speed up the paperwork, OpenAI and PNNL introduced DraftNEPABench. 

In the computer world, a "benchmark" is a tough test used to measure how well software performs. DraftNEPABench is a specific test designed to see how well AI software can help write NEPA documents. 

Instead of regular chatbots, this benchmark tests **AI coding agents**. These are smart, virtual software programs. They live entirely inside computersâ€”they are not physical machines of any kind. When given a complex problem, an AI coding agent can actually write computer code to solve it. 

## How the AI Software Works

The AI does not just guess what to write. It acts as a high-speed digital research assistant for the scientists. Here is how the technical process works:

*   **Reading the Data:** The AI looks at huge spreadsheets of data about local air quality, water flow, and soil types.
*   **Writing the Code:** The AI coding agent writes small computer scripts (usually in a language like Python). These scripts instantly organize and calculate the data.
*   **Drafting the Text:** Using the organized data, the AI writes a rough draft of the environmental report. It explains the numbers in plain English.
*   **Human Review:** The AI does not make final decisions. Human experts read the AI's draft, check the facts, and make sure the document is perfect.

## The Impact: Faster Infrastructure Reviews

The results of the DraftNEPABench testing are very exciting. By using AI coding agents, government teams can reduce the time it takes to draft NEPA documents by up to 15 percent. 

While 15 percent might not sound huge, it makes a massive difference in the real world. If a permit usually takes three years to write, saving 15 percent cuts over five months off the timeline. 

Because of this AI software, new bridges, wind farms, and public transit systems can be built faster. It saves taxpayers money and helps modernize the country's infrastructure without skipping important environmental checks.

## Practical Takeaways

*   **A Powerful Partnership:** OpenAI and PNNL are working together to bring modern AI software into the federal government.
*   **The Tech Tool:** DraftNEPABench is a testing system. It measures how accurately AI models can help write complex federal environmental permits.
*   **Software, Not Hardware:** This technology relies on AI coding agents. These are purely digital software programs that write code to process data faster.
*   **Huge Time Savings:** Using these AI agents can cut the NEPA drafting time by up to 15 percent.
*   **Better Infrastructure:** By speeding up the paperwork, the United States can build clean energy projects and repair old roads much sooner.
